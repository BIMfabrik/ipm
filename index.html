The problem

BIM requirements are often disconnected.

Owners, project teams and operators work with different documents, tools and responsibilities. IPM explores how these fragments can become one connected decision model.

01

Owner information ordering

Start with required business use cases, then derive the information, validation and delivery package.

02

Roles and capabilities

Connect processes to responsibilities and skills instead of defining generic BIM titles.

03

IDS and model checking

Translate information needs into structured, checkable rules and evidence.

04

BIM2FM handover

Link operational processes to asset data, source systems, quality rules and CAFM outputs.

A portfolio project, not just a visualization.

The prototype demonstrates how I approach complex information-management problems: define the operational purpose, connect processes and responsibilities, make requirements testable, and communicate the result in a form that decision-makers can navigate.

The next development steps include explicit relations, KBOB and FM process mappings, role and skill-gap analysis, and scenario-based generation of information requirements.
