Who is responsible?

Titles such as BIM Manager or Information Manager are not enough. Responsibility becomes useful only when it is linked to a process, decision, output and acceptance point.

Derive roles from the work

Start with the selected use cases and required processes. For each step, define who owns the decision, who produces the information, who validates it and who accepts the outcome.

Use case→Process→Decision→Responsible role→Evidence

Questions that matter

  • Who can approve the information requirement?
  • Who creates and updates the information?
  • Who checks technical and business quality?
  • Who resolves conflicts and exceptions?
  • Who accepts the final delivery into operation?
Better than a generic role list: A responsibility map tied to actual outputs and milestones.

Organisational value

This makes gaps, overlaps and unrealistic expectations visible. It also allows organisations to decide whether a capability belongs internally, to a project partner or to a specialist service.
