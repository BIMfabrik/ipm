How does FM benefit?

FM benefits when operational processes define information needs before handover, not when incomplete models arrive at the end of construction.

Start from the FM process

Maintenance, inspections, space management, energy reporting and portfolio planning each need different assets, identifiers and properties. Those needs should shape project delivery from the beginning.

FM process→Asset type→Required data→Source and validation→CAFM output

What improves

  • Only operationally relevant assets and attributes are requested.
  • Identifiers remain stable across BIM, documents and CAFM.
  • Data quality can be checked before final handover.
  • Responsibility for updates and acceptance is clear.
  • Future changes can be traced across systems.
Core principle: FM handover is a process design problem before it is a file-export problem.

From delivery to operational readiness

The graph connects FM outcomes to use cases, processes, information objects, products and actors. That makes readiness measurable and gives project teams a shared picture of what “complete” means.
