Post 01 · The problem

Why static requirements fail.

A requirement written in a document may be correct and still be unusable. The problem is not the sentence. The problem is the missing connection to purpose, responsibility, evidence and acceptance.

A document is not a decision model

Typical AIA/EIR documents contain model formats, attributes, naming rules and delivery dates. But teams still ask: Why is this information needed? Who creates it? Who checks it? What happens when it is missing?

Requirement→Use case→Responsible process→Evidence→Acceptance

What gets lost

  • The operational reason behind the requirement.
  • The role accountable for producing and validating the information.
  • The dependency between one requirement and another.
  • The impact of a change on downstream tools and FM processes.
IPM idea: Treat requirements as connected objects, not isolated paragraphs.

Practical implication

A static document can remain the contractual output. The working model behind it should be structured, linked and queryable. That allows the project to generate focused views for owners, planners, contractors and operators without duplicating the logic.
